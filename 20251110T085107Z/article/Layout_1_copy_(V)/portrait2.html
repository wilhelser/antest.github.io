Holiday, Decorating Policies

At Wexford Apartments, we encourage our residents to decorate for the holidays. We do

ask, however, that you keep the following policies in mind when decorating: • Please use

picture hangers to hang decorations and garland. • Use care not to block hallways, exits or

exit routes. • Please refrain from using window stickers or decals. • Please ensure that lights

are not directed into other homes. • Burning candles and other incendiary devices are not

permitted. Thank you for your cooperation!

Healthier Holiday Cooking Tips

A generation or two ago, people ate more food with a lot of fat, but they still weighed

less than people weigh today. It was probably because they were involved in physical work

instead of sitting at a desk. Or maybe they ate less every day. Whatever the cause, tweaking

Grandma‘s recipes is a great idea. Some examples:

• Refrigerate the gravy and scoop off the hardened fat before reheating and serving.

• Use less bread in turkey dressing and more onions, garlic, celery and vegetables. Moisten

with chicken or vegetable broth and applesauce.

• Make mashed potatoes with skim milk, chicken broth, garlic powder and Parmesan

cheese instead of whole milk and butter. Some recipes call for fat-free sour cream.

• Try a new recipe for eggnog: four bananas, 1 1/2 cups skim milk, 1 1/2 cups plain nonfat

yogurt, 1/4 teaspoon rum extract and some ground nutmeg. Puree the ingredients and

sprinkle the nutmeg on top.

• For dessert, make pumpkin pie using two egg whites for each whole egg and replace

cream with fat-free evaporated milk. Replace heavy cream in cheesecakes and cream pies

with evaporated milk. Top cakes with powdered sugar, fresh fruit or fruit sauce.

• Remove the skin from roasted turkey before eating it or placing it on a serving dish.

Make Your Table the Center Of Attention

Get fresh with your centerpieces by using fresh fruit and gourds to create a festive, stylish

holiday decoration. It‘s easy, just go to your local farmer‘s market or produce section of the

grocery store. Pick fruit that is appealing to the eye, in a variety of colors, sizes and textures.

Clean your fruit well and polish it with vegetable oil to add gloss. Arrange fruit in holiday

bowls by placing a small cereal bowl upside down in the bottom of a larger bowl and placing

fruit around the bowl. Accent with holly, ivy or other greens. Rotate the fruit daily to prevent

rot or spotting and replace the fruit weekly. Enjoy!

Silent Nights

We understand that the holiday season is a time for celebration. During this joyous season,

we ask that you please show consideration to your neighbors who may prefer to celebrate

quietly. Please remind all guests to keep their voices down and turn stereo, television and

car radio volumes to low. Your neighbors will appreciate your thoughtfulness and your

courtesy will ensure we all enjoy our silent nights. Thank you for your assistance!
